Lasagna Recipe

Description

A classic lasagna is a rich, comforting Italian casserole featuring alternating layers of wide flat pasta, a savory meat or marinara sauce, and a creamy cheese blend. It is baked until hot and bubbly, then sliced into decadent, hearty squares.

To prepare it, you first brown ground meat (like beef or Italian sausage) with onions and garlic, simmering it with crushed tomatoes and Italian seasonings. Next, you create a rich cheese layer by mixing ricotta, eggs, Parmesan, and mozzarella. Finally, you assemble the dish in a baking pan by stacking layers of noodles, sauce, and the cheese mixture, finishing with a generous topping of mozzarella before baking.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 teaspoons Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 12 lasagna noodles, cooked and drained
  • 15 ounces ricotta cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, cook ground meat with onion and garlic until browned. Drain excess fat.
  3. Add crushed tomatoes, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  4. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with eggs and Parmesan cheese.
  5. In a baking dish, layer noodles, meat sauce, and ricotta mixture. Repeat layers.
  6. Top with shredded mozzarella cheese.
  7. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes until bubbly and golden.
  8. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.
